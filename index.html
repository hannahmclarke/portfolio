About

The engineer under the design system.

Most design-system conversations are about the components people can see. I tend to work a layer below that — on the build, release, testing and tooling that decide whether a system is actually adoptable: whether a component ships reliably, whether an upgrade is painless, whether a regression is caught before it reaches a consuming app.

Over the last couple of years I've owned that layer for a multi-package design system — migrating its build system, standing up its CI/CD, introducing visual-regression testing, and building the developer tooling around it — while also shipping components and, more recently, the AI tooling that helps both engineers and designers stay on-system. I like the problems that sit between disciplines: build engineering, DX, and now applied AI.

I came into engineering the long way round — from nearly a decade in widening-access education — and I care about widening that door for others. I've taught a Code First Girls intro to Python, built and delivered a free online course on Storybook for React, and spoken on panels about women in tech and changing careers.
